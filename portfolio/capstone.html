Capstone - Nampa Citizen Survey

November 2022 - Present
Citizen Surveys Survey Research Design Statistical Analysis

I was not overly excited to find a Capstone project to meet my BYU MPA graduation requirements (sorry, Dr. Ruddle). After a year and a half of pro-bono work and internships, I felt pretty confident/desirous to go out and, uh, increase my revenues. I had to dig deep and ask myself, "Where do I want to have an impact?" and "What kind of project will I have genuine motivation for?"

I settled upon reaching out to a familiar face at the administrative office of my hometown and asking if he could use any help. As a teenager, my passion for local government was kindled as a lifeguard and swim lesson instructor at our local community pools—I was lucky to have worked with dedicated public servants. I felt charged by the thought of returning to my roots and seeing into the heart of the organization that inspired me. It didn't hurt that Nampa is the best-run city in the country.

I successfully connected with Nampa's Chief of Staff—Rick Hogoboam—and expressed my interest in serving the city. Amidst a brainstorming of ideas, he asked how I would feel about trying to help redo Nampa's Citizen Survey. The potential impact of this project was evident to me, and a class I had taken in the winter semester of 2022, Program Evaluation, gave me skills and even a certification to contribute.

The Project

So far, so good! We are currently in the early process of drafting new questions and reviewing relevant literature. Expect an update on this web page no later than December 31st.
