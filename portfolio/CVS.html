CVS Health - Program Evalution Proposal

January 2022 - April 2022
Recidivism Survey Research Design Second-Chance Program

In my second semester of graduate school at BYU, I completed Program Evaluation with Dr. Eva Witesman. In this class, we received assignments to work with community partners and run through a program evaluation of one of their services. As you likely guessed, I connected with CVS Health. I was delighted to work on a program with immense gravity/impact: Second Chance.

CVS's Second Chance program aims to reduce levels of recidivism through job opportunities coupled with coaching, counseling, education services, and skill training. The program is large and complex but provides at least one key approach to addressing recidivism: CVS has elected to partner primarily with faith-based institutions.

The sad reality is that most justice-impacted individuals will return to criminal behavior. Addiction treatment, counseling, community involvement, church attendance, level of education, and employment are all statistically significant variables in lower rates of individual recidivism, at least in the short-term, but only sustained church attendance is shown to have a long-term effect. Our research suggests that CVS Health is the first corporation to lean into this finding by connecting with individuals most likely to succeed in their program.

There have been many aspects of this project that shaped my education. Most noteworthy is the enjoyment I found in working on designing statistically valid pre- and post-survey tests for Second Chance participants.
